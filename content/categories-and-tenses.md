# Categories and Tenses

Why is the typical way of learning grammar so complicated? One reason is that using three dimensions—moods, aspects, and tenses—creates a complex matrix that's hard to keep in mind when actually speaking. This guide aims to simplify things by focusing on just two dimensions—categories and tenses. That way, a simple table can be used to describe everyday Spanish grammar.

Let's look at the categories and tenses in English to get a feel for them. Note that while familiar grammatical terms are used, some have been adapted to better achieve the goal of simplifying Spanish grammar.

| ---------------------- | --------------------- | -------------- | ------------------ |
| Indicative             | You were (completed)  | You are        | You're going to be |

| Conditional            | You would have been   | —              | You would be       |
| Hypothetical           | If you had been       | If you were    | When you are       |
