# El Acento / La Tilde

Spanish has predictable stress rules. When the natural stress follows the rules, no accent mark is needed. When it breaks the rules, an accent mark (tilde) shows where to stress.

## Rule 1: Words Ending in Vowel, -n, or -s

Stress the **penultimate** (second-to-last) syllable.

| Word | Syllables | Stressed |
|------|-----------|----------|
| casa | ca-sa | CA-sa |
| libro | li-bro | LI-bro |
| hablan | ha-blan | HA-blan |
| lunes | lu-nes | LU-nes |

## Rule 2: Words Ending in Other Consonants

Stress the **last** syllable.

| Word | Syllables | Stressed |
|------|-----------|----------|
| ciudad | ciu-dad | ciu-DAD |
| comer | co-mer | co-MER |
| papel | pa-pel | pa-PEL |

## Rule 3: Breaking the Rules

When a word doesn't follow these patterns, an accent mark shows the stressed syllable.

| Word | Syllables | Why? |
|------|-----------|------|
| café | ca-fé | ends in vowel, but stress is on last |
| también | tam-bién | ends in -n, but stress is on last |
| canción | can-ción | ends in -n, but stress is on last |
| habló | ha-bló | ends in vowel, but stress is on last |
| árbol | ár-bol | ends in consonant, but stress is on penultimate |
| lápiz | lá-piz | ends in consonant, but stress is on penultimate |
| fácil | fá-cil | ends in consonant, but stress is on penultimate |
| teléfono | te-lé-fo-no | ends in vowel, but stress is on third-to-last |
| página | pá-gi-na | ends in vowel, but stress is on third-to-last |
| difícil | di-fí-cil | ends in consonant, but stress is on penultimate |

## Rule 4: Distinguishing Homonyms

Some words are spelled the same but have different meanings. An accent mark distinguishes them.

| Without Accent | With Accent |
|----------------|-------------|
| tu (your) | tú (you) |
| el (the) | él (he) |
| mi (my) | mí (me) |
| se (pronoun) | sé (I know) |
| si (if) | sí (yes) |
| mas (but, uncommon) | más (more) |
| te (pronoun) | té (tea) |
| esta (this) | está (it is) |
| que (that) | qué (what) |

## Rule 5: Breaking Diphthongs

When two vowels that would normally form a diphthong (one syllable) are pronounced as separate syllables, an accent mark is used.

| Word | Syllables | Without Accent Would Be |
|------|-----------|------------------------|
| país | pa-ís | pais (one syllable) |
| caída | ca-í-da | cai-da (two syllables) |
